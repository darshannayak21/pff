Large scale projects

BlueTrust: AI-Driven Satellite Monitoring for Ecological Restoration

BlueTrust is a smart restoration monitoring platform that combines Sentinel-2 satellite imagery with advanced machine learning to track vegetation health and restoration progress across India’s critical blue carbon ecosystems. It automatically identifies and monitors registered restoration sites using NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index), applying temporal ML models to detect meaningful ecological change over time. The system computes percentage improvements in NDVI, classifies vegetation health into defined bands (e.g., Dense, Moderate, Sparse), and filters out noise from seasonal or lighting variations to ensure accurate assessments. Users can compare past vs. current satellite snapshots, draw or define custom regions, and save progress data for future audits. A blockchain-based verification layer anchors all restoration events, ensuring transparency, traceability, and credibility in credit issuance and ESG reporting. BlueTrust brings scientific precision to large-scale restoration by enabling data-driven impact measurement, robust tracking of environmental gains, and a verifiable audit trail for nature-based carbon solutions.

Football GNN

Built an end-to-end football analytics pipeline that harnesses Graph Neural Networks to turn player interactions into meaningful signal. By constructing position-aware player graphs across 72 competitions and training a GraphSAGE model, my system creates high-dimensional, role-specific player embeddings from diverse event streams. Each player node is enriched with detailed action features, while edges prioritize both intra-position and cross-position links for tactical depth. The architecture normalizes heterogeneous data—passes, shots, duels, keeper actions—allowing true role resolution and retrieval of comparable players. This approach enables position-corrected similarity for recruitment and transparent diagnostics, pushing GNNs as a universal blueprint for any domain where relationships drive outcomes—from sports and healthcare to fraud and supply chain networks

Data science and analysis

IoT-Botnet Traffic Analysis & Preprocessing for Cybersecurity Applications I worked on the UNSW_2018_IoT_Botnet dataset, a highly imbalanced and complex cybersecurity dataset containing over 3.6 million rows and 46 features, collected from real IoT device traffic. This project required advanced data engineering steps including multi-source CSV merging, deduplication, and bidirectional imputation (forward and backward fill) to address missing values. I performed feature pruning by dropping non-informative columns like pkSeqID, category, and subcategory, and applied label encoding on all categorical variables using a column-wise approach with reusable encoders. This extensive preprocessing pipeline ensured the dataset was balanced, noise-free, and machine learning-ready, especially for intrusion detection systems (IDS) and network traffic classification tasks. The analysis provided insights into malicious vs. benign traffic patterns, laying the foundation for building robust anomaly detection or supervised classification models in real-world IoT networks.
